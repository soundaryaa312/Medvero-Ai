Hello everyone. Today, I am excited to show you MedVero AI, an intelligent safety verification layer designed to validate medication-related advice generated by general-purpose AI assistants before patients or caregivers act on it.

[0:20 — WHAT IT IS]
MedVero AI is a multi-agent system built using the Google ADK 2.0 framework, belonging to the Agents for Good track. It acts as an independent validator that parses medication claims, checks for safety issues, and retrieves clinical evidence to ensure patient well-being.

[1:00 — ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let's look at the workflow architecture. The request starts at the START node and is immediately scanned by our Security Checkpoint node to scrub PII and check for prompt injections. (pause) Once cleared, it routes to the lead Orchestrator Agent. The Orchestrator delegates to four specialized sub-agents via AgentTools: first, the ClaimsExtractor extracts medication names, dosages, and patient history; next, the EvidenceVerifier retrieves clinical guidelines from our reference database; then, the DrugSafety agent validates drug-drug interactions and dosage safety using MCP server tools; and finally, the ReportGenerator calculates a confidence score and compiles the structured safety report. (pause) If the verification confidence falls below seventy percent, the graph triggers a human clinician review pause before outputting the final report.

[3:00 — SECURITY & MCP]

Security is paramount in digital health. Our Security Checkpoint handles PII redaction and injection checks, writing audit logs in real-time. (pause) Meanwhile, our custom Model Context Protocol server exposes tools like validate_dosage and check_drug_interactions to ensure we always verify claims against hard clinical limits.
